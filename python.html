Data Analysis using Python

These projects demonstrate my Python proficiency, focusing on handling complex data sets. They highlight my skills in data exploration and visualization, utilizing Pandas for data manipulation and Seaborn and Matplotlib for creating insightful charts and graphs. These experiences underscore my ability to derive actionable insights and present data in a clear, impactful manner.

USA Marathon Data Analysis

The primary aim of the project was to investigate how distance and weather conditions affected athlete speeds in marathons held across the USA in 2020. The dataset contained a broad spectrum of information, which required thorough cleaning to isolate the relevant data and remove unnecessary columns and rows. After preparing the data, I employed the Seaborn library to generate a series of visualizations. These graphs effectively illustrated the differences in average speeds between male and female athletes across various marathon events, revealing how performance varied with distance and weather conditions.

Diwali Sales Analysis

The goal of this project was to analyze item sales during the Diwali festival in India. I utilized the Seaborn library to examine buyer demographics, segmenting them by gender and age groups. I further analyzed sales data to identify the top 10 states by order volume and total sales. Additionally, I considered marital status, occupation, product type, and product category. The analysis concluded that women aged 26-35 from Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Karnataka, working in IT, healthcare, and aviation sectors, are more likely to purchase products in the Food, Clothing, and Electronics categories.
